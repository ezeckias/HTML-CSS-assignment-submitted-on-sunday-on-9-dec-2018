Birds, also known as Aves,are a

group of endothermic vertebrates,

characterized by feathers,

toothless beaked,

the laying of hard-shelled eggs

a high metabolic rate,

a four-chambered heart,

and a strong yet lightweight skeleton.

Birds live worldwide and

range in size from the 5 cm (2 in)

bee hummingbird to the 2.75 m (9 ft.) ostrich.

They rank as the world's

most numerically-successful

class of tetrapod,

with approximately ten thousand living species,

more than half of these being passerines,

sometimes known as perching birds.
